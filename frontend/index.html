Reading the results

Breakeven WR = max_loss ÷ (max_loss + total_credit). Below the matrix, we show what your breakeven WR is at the chosen credit/distance combo. Any cell with WR ≥ breakeven is profitable.

Strategy comparison: if Pullback ≥ MeanRev WR with FEWER trades, the trend filter is adding value — it skips the bad signals (selling calls into a rip, selling puts into a flush). In a benign regime (like our test window), the difference is small. In a trending regime, it's large.

Timeframe comparison: 5m fires the most signals but each has the smallest expected move. 15m / 30m fire fewer, cleaner signals. 60m may be too sparse to grind out P&L.

Tail-event blind spot: our 60-day data has no 4%+ days. A real Operation Midnight Hammer day would put EVERY strategy red. The matrix can't show that without out-of-sample crash data.

How these backtests work

Iron Condor (1 trade per day)

Data the backtest can see at decision time: only the first 15 minutes of price action after 09:30 ET (the observation window) plus prior-day ATR. No lookahead.

  1. 09:30-09:45 ET — record observation high/low
  2. 09:45 ET — classify regime (volatile vs non-volatile based on obs_range / D1 ATR)
  3. 09:45 ET — place IC strikes:
    • "Projected H/L" mode: short strikes at our indicator's projected day high/low (volatility-based forecast from obs window)
    • "Distance %" mode: short strikes at obs midpoint ± N% (matches tastytrade-style "1% OTM" placements)
  4. 09:45 → 16:00 ET — walk forward bar-by-bar:
    • If any bar's high ≥ short_call OR low ≤ short_put → BREACH = max loss (close at strike)
    • If neither at 16:00 ET → BOTH HELD = max profit (let expire OTM)

Credit is approximated as credit_pct × wing (since historical 0DTE chain data isn't available). The bound-hold rate is exact.

Wave Trading (multiple trades per day)

Data at entry: only the indicator state (RSI, Stoch %K/%D, WVF) at the signal bar. No lookahead.

  1. Signal fires when Stoch %K crosses %D from above 60 (sell-call) or below 40 (sell-put), 6-bar cooldown between same-direction signals — matches Pine v0.5 deployed indicator
  2. Place spread at signal bar's close: short strike at entry_price ± distance_pct%, long strike at short ± wing_width
  3. Walk forward bar-by-bar in the same session:
    • If short strike breached → BREACH = max loss (close at strike)
    • If price moves favorably by favorable_move_pct → MANAGED = take profit_target_pct of credit
    • If neither at 16:00 ET → EXPIRE OTM = max profit (let expire)

Honest simplification: the "favorable_move_pct" parameter approximates option premium decay via underlying SPX moves. Real wave trading watches the spread's mid-price for the 25-30% target. Without historical 0DTE option chain data, this is the cleanest proxy.

What the backtest CAN'T model accurately

  • Live option premiums — uses fixed credit_pct% assumption rather than actual chain prices
  • Slippage / fill quality — real spreads fill at mid ± a few cents
  • Pin risk — last hour of 0DTE has unique gamma dynamics
  • Margin / BPR changes — real account-level constraints
  • Behavioral discipline — backtest closes at 25% target mechanically; you might hold longer or close earlier
  • Macro events — Trump-style headlines that move SPX 1%+ in 2 minutes are in the data but our predictor doesn't read news

Treat backtest results as "directionally indicative" not "guaranteed P&L". Validate live with paper trading first.
